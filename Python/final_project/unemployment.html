COVID Cases vs. Unemployment Rate

Questions: Here I wanted to see the impact of the outbreak of COVID on the daily lives of American citizens, and their finical situations so that I could compare to the performance of Stock Market Indexes.

Findings: By graphing the monthly unemployment rate vs the daily new COVID cases, I was able to find that from the first of March (data collected pre-covid) to the first of April (directly following the COVID outbreak in US), the unemployment rate rose by 10.4% (4.4% in January, and 14.8% in April). I suspect this sharp rise was due to the restrictions imposed by local governments, and the shuttering of small businesses, as large companies boomed (see Findings in Stock Market). This data reflects the current state of the economy much better than the stock market. As seen in the data, the unemployment rate has not yet recovered to pre-COVID levels, with a rate of 4.4% in March 2020 and 5.8% in May 2021 (June has not been released). Interestingly, I found that the April 2020 unemployment rate was the highest reported level in FRED's database (data is available since 1948).
